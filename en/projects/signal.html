Measurement of Weak Electrical Signals

Weak electrical signal measurement system design based on Arduino embedded development

Abstract

This paper addresses the issues of low sensitivity and susceptibility to interference in weak electrical signal measurement by designing a measurement system based on Arduino embedded development. The system utilizes a suspension wire structure and optical lever principle to achieve physical amplification, combined with signal conditioning circuits and embedded processing for data acquisition and computation. Through normalization algorithms and multi-level noise reduction methods, measurement stability and anti-interference capability are improved. The paper analyzes and evaluates the system structure, hardware implementation, software algorithms, calibration methods, as well as error sources and uncertainty, and verifies system performance through experimental schemes. Additionally, the system is evaluated from a cost perspective, demonstrating good economic viability. Finally, the application scenarios and limitations of the system are discussed. Results show that the system can achieve stable measurement of weak electrical signals and possesses certain practical value.

Keywords: Arduino; weak electrical signal; transimpedance amplifier; PSD sensor; uncertainty evaluation
